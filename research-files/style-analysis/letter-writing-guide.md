# Letter Writing Style Guide for Career Campus AI

## Priority 1: User's Own Voice

When a student has uploaded previous letters (cover letters, application letters), the AI MUST:
1. Read those uploaded letters via `getCoverLetterExamples()`
2. Analyze the student's natural writing style
3. Mirror that voice in every new letter generated

### Style Dimensions to Detect and Mirror

**Opening Style**
- Formal: "I am writing to apply for..."
- Conversational: "I'm excited to apply..."
- Direct: "Your advertisement for... caught my attention"
- Story-driven: "Ever since I completed my first internship..."

**Sentence Length and Rhythm**
- Short punchy sentences vs. longer flowing ones
- Mix of simple and complex structures
- Use of transition words (furthermore, additionally, moreover)

**Vocabulary Level**
- Technical jargon density
- Formal vs. accessible language
- Industry-specific terminology usage
- Word choice patterns (e.g., consistently using "endeavour" vs. "try")

**Self-Presentation Style**
- Humble: "I believe I could contribute..."
- Confident: "My skills directly align with..."
- Evidence-based: "During my placement at X, I achieved..."
- Aspirational: "This role represents my ideal next step..."

**Closing Style**
- Formal: "Yours faithfully / Yours sincerely"
- Enthusiastic: "I look forward to the opportunity..."
- Action-oriented: "I would welcome the chance to discuss..."
- Grateful: "Thank you for considering my application"

## Priority 2: Professional Standards (Non-Negotiable)

Regardless of the student's voice, these are always correct:
- British English spelling (organisation, colour, behaviour, programme)
- Proper date format: 15 June 2026
- Correct salutation based on known name
- Clear paragraph structure: intro, body (2-3 paragraphs), closing
- 300-420 words (not too short, not too long)
- No placeholder brackets like [Name] or [Company]
- No grammatical errors, even if the samples contain them

## Priority 3: Zambian Context

- Reference local universities (UNZA, CBU, Mulungushi)
- Mention relevant local context where natural
- Use appropriate formality level for Zambian business culture
- Reference WIL/Industrial Attachment as common terminology
